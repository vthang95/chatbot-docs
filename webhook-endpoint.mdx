---

Out webhook implementation follows the [Standard Webhooks](https://www.standardwebhooks.com/) specification.
You can find the SDKs for most popular programming languages on the [SDKs page](https://github.com/standard-webhooks/standard-webhooks).


# Get Started

<Info>
  We do not offer sandbox envrionment during development.
</Info>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a new webhook">
    Go to [Webhooks](https://platform.minostack.com/webhooks) and click on `Create Webhook` button to create new webhook.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Specify your endpoint URL">
    Specify your endpoint URL in the `Endpoint URL` field. We will sent a POST request to this URL when the webhook is triggered.
    <Tip>
      <b>Local development?</b><br /><br />
      You can use tool like [ngrok](https://ngrok.com/) to tunnel webhook events to your local development envrionment. This will allow you to test your webhook handlers without deploying them to live server.<br /><br />
      <CodeBlock>
        ngrok http 8080
      </CodeBlock>
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Specify the secret">
    Specify the secret in the `Secret` field. This is used to verify the authenticity of the webhook request.
    Secret accept only alphanumeric characters with a length of at least 24 characters and maximum of 64 characters.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Specify the events">
    Specify the events you want to receive. You can only add one event at a time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Subscribe to events">
    After click on `Create` button, you will be received a [challenge request](/api-reference/challenge). You must response `challenge_id` back to verify your valid webhook.
  </Step>
</Steps>
